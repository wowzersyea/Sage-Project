💊 Stewardship Highlights

The Role of Breastfeeding in Modulating Antimicrobial Resistance in Neonates: A Systematic Review
Pediatric Research, March 21, 2026 | https://doi.org/10.1038/s41390-026-04902-y
PDF: Available (Open Access)

  • Access: OPEN ACCESS

  • Design: Systematic review, n=22 studies, 2015-2025 literature

  • Methods: Selection of studies published between 2015 and 2025 focusing on neonatal AMR and ARG transfer, with 22 studies meeting inclusion criteria after quality assessment

  • Key Findings: Breast milk actively contributes to neonatal gut microbiome development and serves as a source of ARG transfer, while exclusive breastfeeding and bioactive compounds promote beneficial colonization and limit pathogenic bacteria

  • Discussion: Breastfeeding has a dual role - both as a source of antimicrobial resistance genes and containing bioactive components that suppress ARG transmission and promote beneficial colonization

  • Limitations: Need for longitudinal, mechanistic studies to determine causal relationships and long-term effects

  • Clinical Implications: Breastfeeding-centered interventions, combined with maternal health optimization and antibiotic stewardship, represent promising avenues to mitigate AMR risks
